About This Tool

ABOUT

CERTAINTY is a tool designed to make fact checking sources straightforward and easy. By breaking down the process into to quick and understandable questions, you can check any source with ease.

CERTAINTY puts all of the resources at your fingertips and guides you through the process, then, it uses your answers to determine the likelyhood that your source is misinformation or biased. Additionally, it explains how the conclusion is reached, allowing you to understand why your source is trustworthy or not.

Tips For Finding Sources:

Simplify the topic into its most vital parts like time, person/subject, location, and/or event.

For example, you could use a time, location, and subject to search "New California tax law,"

You could also try a person and a subject to search, for example, "Bill Gates philanthropy"

When searching for professional research or studies, simply search for the subject followed by "study," such as "Crows intelligence study"

It can also be beneficial to search for topics on sites known to be trustworthy. For example, Wikipedia has more than 7 million pages each with a section dedicated to citing the sources used, and Britannica has over 100 full time editors reviewing their encyclopedia.

For breaking news stories, Reuters is a notably reliable news agency remarked for being very unbiased. Generally, however, the more sources you can find for a story, the better, so if you have the time, check other news sites as well.
